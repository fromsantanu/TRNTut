# 📖 Chapter 26: Model Evaluation (Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1)

---

## 🔷 1. Why Model Evaluation is Important

After building a model, we must ask:

👉 **“How good is this model?”**

---

### 🧠 One-Line Idea

👉
“Evaluation tells us whether our model is making correct decisions.”

---

## 🔷 2. Example Problem (Simple Case)

Let’s take a **spam detection model**:

* Spam = Positive
* Not Spam = Negative

---

### 🧠 Example Predictions

| Email         | Actual   | Predicted  |
| ------------- | -------- | ---------- |
| Offer now     | Spam     | Spam ✅     |
| Hello friend  | Not Spam | Spam ❌     |
| Win prize     | Spam     | Spam ✅     |
| Meeting today | Not Spam | Not Spam ✅ |

---

👉 Now we evaluate performance

---

## 🔷 3. Confusion Matrix (Base Concept)

---

### 💡 Definition

A **confusion matrix** shows:

👉 Correct and incorrect predictions

---

### 🧠 Table

|                 | Predicted Spam | Predicted Not Spam |
| --------------- | -------------- | ------------------ |
| Actual Spam     | TP             | FN                 |
| Actual Not Spam | FP             | TN                 |

---

### 💡 Meaning

* TP (True Positive) → Correct spam
* TN (True Negative) → Correct non-spam
* FP (False Positive) → Wrongly marked spam
* FN (False Negative) → Missed spam

---

## 🔷 4. Accuracy

---

### 💡 Definition

👉 **How many predictions are correct overall**

---

$$\text{Accuracy} = \frac{TP + TN}{TP + TN + FP + FN}$$

---

### 🧠 Example

If 90 out of 100 are correct:

👉 Accuracy = 90%

---

### ⚠️ Problem

Accuracy can be misleading

---

### 🧠 Example

* 95 normal emails
* 5 spam

Model predicts all as “normal”

👉 Accuracy = 95% ❌ (but useless)

---

## 🔷 5. Precision

---

### 💡 Definition

👉 **Out of predicted positives, how many are correct**

---

\text{Precision} = \frac{TP}{TP + FP}

---

### 🧠 Example

If model says 10 emails are spam:

* 8 correct → Precision = 80%

---

### 🟡 Use Case

When false alarms are costly

👉 Example:

* Marking important email as spam

---

## 🔷 6. Recall

---

### 💡 Definition

👉 **Out of actual positives, how many were found**

---

$$\text{Recall} = \frac{TP}{TP + FN}$$

---

### 🧠 Example

If there are 10 spam emails:

* Model finds 7 → Recall = 70%

---

### 🟡 Use Case

When missing something is dangerous

👉 Example:

* Disease detection

---

## 🔷 7. F1 Score

---

### 💡 Definition

👉 Balance between precision and recall

---

$$F1 = 2 \times \frac{\text{Precision} \times \text{Recall}}{\text{Precision} + \text{Recall}}$$

---

### 🧠 Example

* Precision = 80%
* Recall = 70%

👉 F1 gives combined performance

---

## 🔷 8. When to Use Which Metric

---

| Metric    | Use When              |
| --------- | --------------------- |
| Accuracy  | Balanced data         |
| Precision | Avoid false positives |
| Recall    | Avoid false negatives |
| F1 Score  | Need balance          |

---

## 🔷 9. Real-World Examples

---

### 🧬 Healthcare

* Disease detection → **Recall important**
  (Missing disease is dangerous)

---

### 📧 Spam Detection

* Precision important
  (Avoid marking real email as spam)

---

### 🤖 General AI Tasks

* Use F1 score

---

## 🔷 10. Simple Analogy

---

### 🎯 Target Practice

* Accuracy → total hits
* Precision → how accurate your hits are
* Recall → how many targets you hit
* F1 → balance of both

---

---

## 🔷 12. Key Takeaway

👉
“Different evaluation metrics measure different aspects of model performance, and choosing the right metric depends on the problem.”
