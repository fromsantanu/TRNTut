# 📖 Chapter 28: Improving Performance

---

## 🔷 1. Why Improvement is Needed

After building and fine-tuning a model, we often see:

* Some wrong predictions
* Inconsistent results
* Lower accuracy than expected

👉 So we ask:

👉 **“How can we make the model better?”**

---

### 🧠 One-Line Idea

👉
“Small changes in training and data can greatly improve model performance.”

---

## 🔷 2. Main Areas of Improvement

---

We can improve performance in three key ways:

1. **Data Quality**
2. **Hyperparameters**
3. **Model Strategy**

---

---

# 🔷 3. Improving Data Quality (Most Important)

---

### 💡 Why Data Matters

👉 “Garbage in → Garbage out”

---

### 🧠 Problems with Bad Data

* Wrong labels
* Missing values
* Irrelevant text

---

### ✅ Solutions

* Clean text (remove noise)
* Fix incorrect labels
* Balance dataset

---

### 🧠 Example

Bad data:
👉 “I love this movie” → labeled negative ❌

Good data:
👉 Correct label → positive ✅

---

## 🔷 4. Handling Imbalanced Data

---

### 💡 Problem

* Too many examples of one class

---

### 🧠 Example

* 95% non-spam
* 5% spam

---

### ❌ Result

Model predicts everything as non-spam

---

### ✅ Solutions

* Oversampling (add minority data)
* Undersampling (reduce majority data)
* Use better evaluation metrics (F1 score)

---

---

# 🔷 5. Hyperparameter Tuning

---

## 🔷 What are Hyperparameters?

👉 Settings that control training process

---

### 🔑 Important Hyperparameters

---

### 🔹 Learning Rate

* Controls how fast model learns

👉 Too high → unstable
👉 Too low → slow

---

### 🔹 Batch Size

* Number of samples processed at once

👉 Small → better learning
👉 Large → faster

---

### 🔹 Number of Epochs

* How many times model sees data

👉 Too few → underfitting
👉 Too many → overfitting

---

### 🧠 Example

* Learning rate = 2e-5 (common for Transformers)
* Epochs = 2–5

---

---

# 🔷 6. Avoiding Overfitting

---

### 💡 What is Overfitting?

👉 Model memorizes training data but fails on new data

---

### 🧠 Example

* Training accuracy = 99%
* Test accuracy = 70% ❌

---

### ✅ Solutions

* Use validation data
* Reduce epochs
* Add regularization
* Use dropout

---

---
