# 📖 Chapter 13: Efficient Transformers (Longformer)

---

## 🔷 1. The Problem with Standard Transformers

Standard Transformers use **self-attention on every word with every other word**.

👉 This creates a big issue:

### ❌ Computation grows very fast

If a sentence has:

* 100 words → 100 × 100 = 10,000 comparisons
* 1000 words → 1,000,000 comparisons 😲

---

### 🧠 Real-Life Analogy

Imagine:

* 100 people in a room
* Everyone talks to everyone

👉 Very noisy and slow

---

## 🔷 2. Why This is a Problem

---

### ❌ Slow processing

### ❌ High memory usage

### ❌ Difficult for long documents

---

### 🧠 Example

Tasks like:

* Reading full research papers
* Legal documents
* Medical reports

👉 Standard Transformer struggles

---

## 🔷 3. What is an Efficient Transformer?

---

### 💡 Definition

Efficient Transformers are designed to:

👉 **reduce computation while still understanding long text**

---

👉 They do this by:

* Limiting unnecessary connections
* Focusing only on important parts

---

## 🔷 4. Longformer: A Key Example

---

👉 Longformer

---

### 💡 What Longformer Does

Instead of full attention:

👉 Uses **selective attention patterns**

---

## 🔷 5. Key Idea: Sparse Attention

---

### 💡 Meaning

Not every word talks to every other word

👉 Only important connections are made

---

### 🧠 Example

Sentence:
👉 *“The boy who was playing in the park is very happy.”*

Instead of connecting all words:

* boy ↔ playing
* boy ↔ happy
* ignore less important links

---

### 🟡 Analogy

In a classroom:

* You don’t talk to everyone
* You talk only to relevant people

---

## 🔷 6. Types of Attention in Longformer

---

### 🟢 1. Local Attention

* Words attend to nearby words

👉 Example:

* “playing” connects to “boy”

---

### 🔵 2. Global Attention

* Important words attend to all others

👉 Example:

* “main subject” connects globally

---

### 🧠 Example

👉 *“The president gave a speech…”*

* “president” gets global attention

---

## 🔷 7. How Longformer Works (Simple Flow)

---

1. Input text is given
2. Apply embeddings + positional encoding
3. Use sparse attention (local + global)
4. Build context
5. Output representation

---

👉 Same idea as Transformer, but **more efficient**

---

## 🔷 8. Why Longformer is Powerful

---

### ✅ Handles very long text

### ✅ Uses less memory

### ✅ Faster than standard Transformer

### ✅ Maintains good accuracy

---

## 🔷 9. Where Longformer is Used

---

### 📄 Long documents

### ⚖️ Legal text analysis

### 🧬 Medical research papers

### 📚 Book summarization

---

### 🧠 Example

👉 Summarizing a 50-page report

Standard Transformer ❌ struggles
Longformer ✅ works well

---

## 🔷 10. Comparison with Standard Transformer

---

| Feature   | Standard Transformer | Longformer |
| --------- | -------------------- | ---------- |
| Attention | Full                 | Sparse     |
| Speed     | Slow (long text)     | Faster     |
| Memory    | High                 | Lower      |
| Long text | Difficult            | Easy       |

---

---

## 🔷 12. Simple Real-Life Analogy

---

### 📞 Phone Calls

* Standard Transformer → everyone calls everyone
* Longformer → only important calls are made

---

👉 Much more efficient

---

## 🔷 13. Key Takeaway

👉
“Efficient Transformers like Longformer reduce unnecessary attention connections, allowing models to process long text faster and with less memory.”
