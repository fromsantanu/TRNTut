# 📖 Chapter 16: Sparse Transformers

---

## 🔷 1. The Core Problem

In a standard Transformer:

👉 **every word attends to every other word**

---

### 🧠 What does this mean?

If a sentence has:

* 100 words → 10,000 connections
* 1000 words → 1,000,000 connections 😲

---

### 🟡 Real-Life Analogy

Imagine a classroom:

* Every student talks to every other student

👉 Too much noise, too slow, inefficient

---

## 🔷 2. What are Sparse Transformers?

---

### 💡 Definition

**Sparse Transformers** reduce the number of connections:

👉 **each word attends only to selected important words, not all words**

---

### 🧠 One-Line Idea

👉
“Focus only on important connections, ignore the rest.”

---

## 🔷 3. Why Sparsity is Needed

---

### ❌ Problems with full attention

* Very slow
* Uses too much memory
* Hard for long sequences

---

### ✅ Solution

* Reduce connections
* Keep only useful ones

---

## 🔷 4. How Sparse Attention Works

---

Instead of:
👉 connecting everything

We do:
👉 selective connections

---

### 🧠 Example Sentence

👉 *“The boy who was playing in the park is very happy.”*

---

### Full Attention:

* Every word ↔ every word

---

### Sparse Attention:

* boy ↔ playing
* boy ↔ happy
* ignore less important links

---

## 🔷 5. Types of Sparse Patterns

---

### 🟢 1. Local Attention

* Words attend to nearby words

👉 Example:

* “playing” connects to “boy”

---

### 🔵 2. Strided Attention

* Words connect at fixed intervals

👉 Example:

* Word 1 → Word 5 → Word 9

---

### 🟡 3. Global Attention

* Important words connect to all

👉 Example:

* “main subject” attends everywhere

---

## 🔷 9. Comparison with Standard Transformer

---

| Feature   | Standard Transformer | Sparse Transformer |
| --------- | -------------------- | ------------------ |
| Attention | Full                 | Partial            |
| Speed     | Slow                 | Faster             |
| Memory    | High                 | Lower              |
| Long text | Difficult            | Easier             |
