# 📖 Chapter 12: Encoder–Decoder Models

---

## 🔷 1. What are Encoder–Decoder Models?

**Encoder–Decoder models** combine both parts of a Transformer:

👉 **Encoder → understands input**
👉 **Decoder → generates output**

---

### 🧠 One-Line Idea

👉
“First understand the input, then generate the correct output.”

---

## 🔷 2. Real Model Example

A well-known model using this approach is:

👉 T5

---

### 💡 What T5 Does

* Converts **one form of text into another**
* Treats everything as a **text-to-text task**

---

### 🧠 Example

Input:
👉 *“Translate English to Hindi: I am happy”*

Output:
👉 *“मैं खुश हूँ”*

---

## 🔷 3. Why Encoder–Decoder Models Are Needed

---

### Problem with Encoder-only:

* Can understand text
* ❌ Cannot generate output

---

### Problem with Decoder-only:

* Can generate text
* ❌ May not fully understand input

---

### Solution:

👉 Combine both for best performance

---

### 🟡 Real-Life Analogy

Translator:

1. Listens carefully (Encoder)
2. Speaks translation (Decoder)

---

## 🔷 4. How They Work (Step-by-Step)

---

### Step 1: Input Sentence

👉 *“Summarize: The movie was long but interesting.”*

---

### Step 2: Encoder Processes Input

* Understands relationships
* Builds context

👉 Example:

* movie ↔ interesting
* long ↔ but

---

### Step 3: Pass Information to Decoder

👉 Encoder output acts like **knowledge base**

---

### Step 4: Decoder Generates Output

👉 Output:
*“Interesting but long movie.”*

---

## 🔷 5. Special Component: Encoder–Decoder Attention

---

### 💡 What is it?

Decoder can **look at encoder output while generating**

---

### 🧠 Example

While generating word “interesting”:

* Decoder checks encoder’s understanding of input

---

### 🟡 Analogy

Student writing answer:

* Keeps looking at question paper

---

## 🔷 6. Types of Tasks Supported

---

### 🟢 1. Translation

👉 English → Hindi

---

### 🟢 2. Summarization

👉 Long text → short text

---

### 🟢 3. Question Answering

👉 Question → Answer

---

### 🟢 4. Text Conversion

👉 Formal → informal
👉 Active → passive

---

## 🔷 7. Advantages of Encoder–Decoder Models

---

### ✅ Strong understanding + generation

### ✅ Accurate outputs

### ✅ Flexible for many tasks

### ✅ Works well for structured problems

---

## 🔷 8. Comparison with Other Models

---

| Model Type      | Role                  | Example |
| --------------- | --------------------- | ------- |
| Encoder         | Understand only       | BERT    |
| Decoder         | Generate only         | GPT     |
| Encoder–Decoder | Understand + Generate | T5      |

---

## 🔷 9. Limitations

---

### ❌ More complex

### ❌ Slower than single models

### ❌ Needs more data

---

## 🔷 10. Visual Intuition

---

👉 *“The teacher explained the lesson clearly.”*

---

### Encoder:

* Understands:

  * teacher ↔ explained
  * lesson ↔ clearly

---

### Decoder:

* Generates output based on this understanding
