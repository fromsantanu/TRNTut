# 📖 Chapter 10: Encoder-Based Models

---

## 🔷 1. What are Encoder-Based Models?

**Encoder-based models** use only the **encoder part** of a Transformer.

👉 Their main job is:

👉 **to understand text deeply (not generate new text)**

---

### 🧠 One-Line Idea

👉
“Encoder models read and understand — they do not write.”

---

## 🔷 2. Real Model Example

A popular encoder-based model is:

👉 BERT

---

### 💡 What BERT Does

* Reads full sentence
* Understands context from both sides
* Creates meaningful representation of text

---

## 🔷 3. Key Feature: Bidirectional Understanding

---

### 💡 What does this mean?

The model reads:

👉 **left + right context together**

---

### 🧠 Example

👉 *“The movie was not good”*

* “not” affects “good”
* Encoder understands this because it sees the full sentence

---

### 🟡 Analogy

Reading a sentence:

* You look at the whole line
* Then understand meaning

---

## 🔷 4. How Encoder-Based Models Work

---

### Step-by-Step Flow

1. Input sentence is given
2. Convert into tokens
3. Apply embeddings + positional encoding
4. Use self-attention to connect words
5. Output = context-rich representation

---

### 🧠 Example

👉 *“The boy is playing football”*

Model understands:

* boy ↔ playing
* playing ↔ football

---

## 🔷 5. Output of Encoder Models

---

### Important Point:

Encoder models do **not generate sentences**

👉 They produce:

👉 **numerical representations (embeddings)**

---

These are then used for tasks like:

* classification
* prediction

---

## 🔷 6. Common Tasks Using Encoder Models

---

### 🟢 1. Text Classification

👉 Example:

* Spam vs Not Spam
* Positive vs Negative sentiment

---

### 🟢 2. Question Answering

👉 Example:

* Input: Paragraph + Question
* Output: Answer from text

---

### 🟢 3. Named Entity Recognition (NER)

👉 Example:

* Identify names, places, dates

---

### 🟢 4. Search and Information Retrieval

👉 Example:

* Matching query with documents

---

## 🔷 7. Why Encoder Models Are Powerful

---

### ✅ Understand full context

### ✅ Capture relationships

### ✅ Handle complex sentences

### ✅ Work efficiently

---

### 🧠 Example

👉 *“Apple released a new product”*

Encoder understands:

* Apple = company (not fruit)

---

## 🔷 8. Other Encoder-Based Models

---

Besides BERT, there are variants like:

* RoBERTa
* DistilBERT

---

### 💡 Why variants?

* Faster
* Smaller
* More accurate

---

## 🔷 9. Encoder vs Decoder (Quick Comparison)

---

| Feature   | Encoder        | Decoder       |
| --------- | -------------- | ------------- |
| Role      | Understand     | Generate      |
| Direction | Bidirectional  | Left to right |
| Output    | Representation | Text          |
| Example   | BERT           | GPT           |

---

## 🔷 10. Limitations of Encoder Models

---

### ❌ Cannot generate text

### ❌ Not suitable for chat or writing

### ❌ Needs additional layers for tasks

---

## 🔷 11. Simple Real-Life Analogy

---

### 📘 Reader vs Writer

* Encoder = Reader (understands text)
* Decoder = Writer (creates text)

---

## 🔷 12. Key Takeaway

👉
“Encoder-based models focus on understanding text by analyzing relationships between words using self-attention, making them ideal for classification and comprehension tasks.”

---
